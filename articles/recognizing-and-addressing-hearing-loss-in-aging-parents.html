Recognizing and Treating Hearing Loss in Aging Parents

We can expect some particular health changes as our family members get older. Hearing loss is one of the most predominant of these health concerns. Around one out of every three people aged 65 to 74 copes with some degree of hearing loss as disclosed by the National Institute of Health. This number increases substantially for individuals over 75, with almost half of all Americans in this age group struggling to hear.

Diligently watching for indications of hearing loss is crucial if you have an aging relative that you’re taking care of. Their quality of life can be improved and managing the symptoms will be more effective with early detection.

How will you know if a senior has hearing loss?

Below are some significant signs that your loved one might be experiencing hearing difficulties:

Asking people to repeat themselves

Does your loved one frequently ask you to repeat yourself? This is a common sign that they might not be hearing clearly, particularly in situations with background noise or multiple speakers.

Cranking the volume up

If they constantly turn up the TV or radio volume so high that it’s uncomfortably loud for others, it could indicate that they are struggling to hear at normal volumes.

Challenges talking to others

Frustrating misunderstandings can occur when your family member meets somebody but can’t hear them even when they are talking clearly.

Disputes stemming from miscommunication

Hearing loss can frequently result in frequent quarrels with friends or family due to miscommunication or the perception that they aren’t listening, when in truth, they simply can’t hear well.

Essential sounds are missed

If your family member isn’t responding to essential sounds like the alarm clock, cell phone notifications, or even car alarms, this is an obvious indication of hearing loss.

Difficulty talking on the phone

Phone conversations can be particularly difficult for seniors, and if there is background noise it becomes even more challenging.

Avoidance of social situations

Your loved one may feel embarrassed about their hearing problems causing them to remove themselves from social contact and often seclude themselves.

The impact of neglected hearing loss

Hearing loss has a profound effect on social interaction, emotional well-being, as well as physical health. Untreated hearing loss can lead to solitude, depression, and a decrease in cognitive function. It’s important to recognize the indications early and get treatment to help your family member maintain their quality of life.

The role of hearing aids

Luckily, hearing loss, particularly age-related hearing loss, can often be successfully managed with the use of hearing aids. These devices can significantly improve the ability to hear and allow seniors to continue participating in their normal activities. Remaining socially active with family, friends, and community is essential for the overall health and well-being of older adults.

Studies done by Johns Hopkins University have connected neglected hearing loss to cognitive decline, memory issues, and an increased danger of dementia and Alzheimer’s disease. Regular use of hearing aids is an essential step in caring for an older relative because hearing aids have been shown to counter or even reverse many of these cognitive risks.

How you can help

You can help a loved one going through hearing loss in several ways. The first thing you should do is to encourage them to get a hearing test from a hearing specialist. Together, you can investigate the available treatment options, including hearing aids.

It’s also essential for your loved one to be proactive about the health of their hearing. Honesty about their hearing difficulties and a willingness to wear their hearing aids consistently is essential. While hearing aids are a substantial investment, they are essential for maintaining your loved one’s overall health and happiness.

By dealing with hearing loss early and assisting your loved one through the process, you can help them regain a vibrant social life. This will allow them to reconnect with friends and family and enjoy the relationships that are so vital to their emotional and mental health. Your family member will be able to keep living a rewarding life with the proper hearing care and your help.
