Summer Hearing Aid Care: Tips for Safe and Effective Use

Summer is a season filled with adventure, outdoor fun, and unforgettable moments. Your hearing aids are essential for staying connected as you engage in activities like lounging by the pool, going to summer music events, exploring hiking paths, or hosting backyard get-togethers. Warmer temperatures and outdoor settings can pose various difficulties for individuals who rely on hearing aids. Guaranteeing your devices function smoothly throughout the warmer months requires a solid grasp of appropriate maintenance and upkeep techniques especially tailored for the summer season.

Common summer challenges for hearing aid users

Each season introduces its own challenges for hearing aid users, and summer’s warm, often humid conditions can put your devices at risk. Typical summer problems to watch out for:

Humidity and moisture

Exposure to moisture from activities involving water, rain, or excessive sweating can present a risk to hearing aids. Extreme moisture levels have the ability to penetrate sensitive electronic parts, which might lead to operational problems or rusting. Exposure to even minimal humidity inside your devices can compromise audio effectiveness or lead to irreversible damage.

Outdoor elements and wind

Powerful winds can cause hearing aids to become loose in your ears or bring particles like dust, sand, and debris into fragile areas, posing more than just a slight inconvenience. Engaging in outdoor pursuits such as gardening, visiting the beach, or playing sports might subject your hearing aids to dust and debris that can affect how well they work.

Heat and sun exposure

Exposing hearing aids to direct sunlight or high temperatures, like leaving them on a car dashboard, can lead to overheating, decreased battery life, or distortion of plastic parts. Intense heat speeds up wear and tear and decreases the longevity of devices.

Tips to safeguard your hearing aids all summer long

With a few sensible habits, you can keep your hearing aids safe, dry, and functioning at their best, regardless of your summer plans are.

1. Safeguard your hearing aids from water

  • Remove Before Swimming or Showering: Always take out your hearing aids before swimming, bathing, or any water exposure. Utilize swim caps or waterproof earplugs to keep your ear canals dry.
  • After being exposed to moisture, make sure to gently dry your devices using a soft microfiber cloth. Avoid using heat-producing devices such as hair dryers for drying purposes.
  • To decrease moisture build-up, leave the battery compartments open and store the hearing aids in a drying case or dehumidifying device for several hours or overnight, allowing them to air out completely.

2. Control perspiration during exercise

  • Consider wearing sweatbands or headbands to absorb extra sweat and shield your ears and hearing devices while exercising or doing tasks outside.
  • Prevent Moisture Buildup: To keep your hearing aids functioning properly, make sure to dry your ears thoroughly after showering or bathing before putting them back in.

3. Ensure a sanitary environment

  • Frequent Cleaning Routine: Clean your hearing aids every day with disinfectant wipes made for electronics or hearing devices to decrease bacteria growth caused by heat and sweat.
  • Regularly Check and Clean Your Devices: It’s important to periodically examine your devices for any accumulation of debris, including dust, dirt, or earwax, and take the necessary measures to eliminate it. Regular cleanings performed by your hearing professional every couple of months can help prevent permanent damage.
  • Remember to put your hearing aids in a cool, dry area when they are not being used. Be sure to keep them away from sunlight and high temperatures.

4. Monitor device function and seek professional help

If you detect differences in how well you hear, any noises coming from your hearing aids, or how long the battery lasts in the hotter season, be certain to reach out to your hearing specialist without hesitation. They are able to perform vital maintenance or repairs in order to ensure that your hearing aids function effectively.

Have a hassle-free summer with well-maintained hearing aids.

Summer is meant for creating memories, not for stressing about your hearing aids. With mindful care and a few easy precautions, your devices can handle the heat, moisture, and outdoor elements, letting you experience every conversation, bird song, and splash with crystal-clear sound.

For professional guidance on protecting your hearing aids or to set up a thorough check-up, book a consultation with a specialist at your earliest convenience. Enjoy your summer to the fullest, with confidence that your hearing aids are ready for any adventure.
